The name Lily

Story and style

Lily is a nature-inspired and vintage name for girls. It is usually linked to the lily flower, giving it a clear meaning without making the name feel too serious. Lily is best understood as an English nature name rather than a name built from ancient personal-name elements.

As a baby name, Lily feels natural, gentle and a little vintage. It is not usually treated as a Biblical, Catholic or traditional saint name. It can stand on its own or sit beside longer related forms such as Lilly, Lillie, Lilian. Because Lily is already short, it does not really need a nickname, though Lil can work casually.

Background notes

Meaning

Lily is most commonly linked to the lily flower. That meaning gives the name a clear little story without making it feel overworked.

Because the meaning is tied to a real-world image, Lily tends to feel more visual and concrete than names with abstract meanings.

Origin

Lily is commonly traced to English flower name. It is associated with English roots.

It is better understood as a word-name from English usage than as a name built from older personal-name elements.

History and background

Lily fits the long tradition of nature names, but it also works for modern parents because short botanical and colour names have become popular again.

Is Lily a religious name?

Lily is not usually considered a Biblical, Catholic or traditional saint name. It is better understood as a nature or word name.

What style of name is Lily?

Lily fits with nature-inspired names and gentle vintage choices. It sits naturally beside names like Daisy, Violet, Rose and Ivy: names that feel related in sound, style or popularity.
