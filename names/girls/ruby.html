The name Ruby

Story and style

Ruby is a nature-inspired and vintage name for girls. It is usually linked to the ruby gemstone, giving it a clear meaning without making the name feel too serious. Ruby is best understood as an English nature name rather than a name built from ancient personal-name elements.

As a baby name, Ruby feels natural, gentle and a little vintage. It is not usually treated as a Biblical, Catholic or traditional saint name. It can stand on its own or sit beside longer related forms such as Rubie. Because Ruby is already short, it does not really need a nickname, though Rue can work casually.

Background notes

Meaning

Ruby is most commonly linked to the ruby gemstone. That meaning gives the name a clear little story without making it feel overworked.

Because the meaning is tied to a real-world image, Ruby tends to feel more visual and concrete than names with abstract meanings.

Origin

Ruby is commonly traced to English gemstone name. It is associated with English or Latin roots.

It is better understood as a word-name from English usage than as a name built from older personal-name elements.

History and background

Ruby fits the long tradition of nature names, but it also works for modern parents because short botanical and colour names have become popular again.

Is Ruby a religious name?

Ruby is not usually considered a Biblical, Catholic or traditional saint name. It is better understood as a nature or word name.

What style of name is Ruby?

Ruby fits with nature-inspired names and gentle vintage choices. It sits naturally beside names like Violet, Lily, Hazel and Ivy: names that feel related in sound, style or popularity.

Nicknames and variations

Ruby is often shortened to Rue. Related forms may include Rubie.
