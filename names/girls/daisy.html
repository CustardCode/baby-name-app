The name Daisy

Story and style

Daisy is a nature-inspired and vintage name for girls. It is usually linked to the daisy flower, giving it a clear meaning without making the name feel too serious. Daisy is best understood as an English nature name rather than a name built from ancient personal-name elements.

As a baby name, Daisy feels natural, gentle and a little vintage. It is not usually treated as a Biblical, Catholic or traditional saint name. Related forms include Daisie. It is often shortened to Daze.

More about Daisy

Background notes

Meaning

Daisy is most commonly linked to the daisy flower. That meaning gives the name a clear little story without making it feel overworked.

Because the meaning is tied to a real-world image, Daisy tends to feel more visual and concrete than names with abstract meanings.

Origin

Daisy is commonly traced to English flower name. It is associated with English roots.

It is better understood as a word-name from English usage than as a name built from older personal-name elements.

History and background

Daisy fits the long tradition of nature names, but it also works for modern parents because short botanical and colour names have become popular again.

Is Daisy a religious name?

Daisy is not usually considered a Biblical, Catholic or traditional saint name. It is better understood as a nature or word name.

What style of name is Daisy?

Daisy fits with nature-inspired names and gentle vintage choices. It sits naturally beside names like Lily, Violet, Rose and Ivy: names that feel related in sound, style or popularity.

Nicknames and variations

Daisy is often shortened to Daze. Related forms may include Daisie.
