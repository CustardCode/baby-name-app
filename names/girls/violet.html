The name Violet

Story and style

Violet is a nature-inspired and vintage name for girls. It is usually linked to the violet flower, giving it a clear meaning without making the name feel too serious. Violet is best understood as an English nature name rather than a name built from ancient personal-name elements.

As a baby name, Violet feels natural, gentle and a little vintage. It is not usually treated as a Biblical, Catholic or traditional saint name. Related forms include Violette, Viola. Nickname options include Vi, Vivi.

Background notes

Meaning

Violet is most commonly linked to the violet flower. That meaning gives the name a clear little story without making it feel overworked.

Because the meaning is tied to a real-world image, Violet tends to feel more visual and concrete than names with abstract meanings.

Origin

Violet is commonly traced to English flower and colour name. It is associated with English or Latin roots.

It is better understood as a word-name from English usage than as a name built from older personal-name elements.

History and background

Violet fits the long tradition of nature names, but it also works for modern parents because short botanical and colour names have become popular again.

What style of name is Violet?

Violet fits with nature-inspired names and gentle vintage choices. It sits naturally beside names like Lily, Daisy, Hazel and Ivy: names that feel related in sound, style or popularity.

Nicknames and variations

Common nickname options include Vi, Vivi. Related forms may include Violette, Viola.
