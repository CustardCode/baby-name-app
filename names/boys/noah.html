The name Noah

Story and style

Noah is a biblical and historic name for boys. It is usually linked with rest or repose, giving it a clear meaning without making the name feel too serious.

For parents, Noah is easy to imagine in everyday life: simple enough for a child, but still polished enough to grow with them. It also has a Biblical background, which may appeal to families who like names with Jewish or Christian tradition. It can stand on its own or sit beside longer related forms such as Noa. Because Noah is already short, it does not really need a nickname, though Noe can work casually.

More about Noah

Background notes

Meaning

Noah is most commonly linked with rest or repose. That meaning gives the name a clear little story without making it feel overworked.

For many families, that meaning sits alongside the name's Hebrew or Biblical background.

Origin

Noah is commonly traced to Hebrew biblical name. It is associated with Hebrew roots.

Its roots are older than modern English usage, which is why the name can feel both familiar and traditional.

History and background

Noah has older roots than many modern baby names, which helps explain why it can feel established rather than trend-made.

Is Noah a religious name?

Noah has a Biblical background, so it may appeal to families who like names with Jewish or Christian tradition.

What style of name is Noah?

Noah has a historic, established feel without sounding overly formal. It sits naturally beside names like Levi, Elijah, Ezra and Isaac: names that feel related in sound, style or popularity.

Nicknames and variations

Noah is often shortened to Noe. Related forms may include Noa.
